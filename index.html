Feeling overwhelmed is common, especially during stressful times. Start by taking a few deep breaths to calm your mind. Break down tasks into smaller steps and focus on one thing at a time. It's also helpful to talk to someone you trust, like a friend, family member, or counselor. If you're consistently feeling overwhelmed, reaching out for professional support can make a huge difference.
If you're feeling consistently sad, anxious, or hopeless, or if you find it hard to function day-to-day (whether academically, socially, or emotionally), speaking with a counselor can help. Therapy is not just for crises - it's for anyone who wants to explore and improve their mental health. It's okay to ask for help when you need it.
Yes, it's completely normal to feel anxious or nervous before important events. A little anxiety can be motivating and help you focus. However, if your anxiety becomes overwhelming or interferes with your ability to prepare, it's important to practice stress-relief techniques, like deep breathing, or seek additional support from a counselor.
Time management is key. Create a balanced schedule that includes study time, social activities, and self-care. Prioritize your tasks and set boundaries to protect your time. Don't hesitate to say "no" when necessary to avoid over-committing. Remember that self-care isn't selfish - it's essential for maintaining long-term productivity and well-being.

Sometimes, just being there for them and letting them know they're not alone can make a big difference. However, it's important to remember that you aren't a therapist, and professional help may be needed if the situation worsens.
